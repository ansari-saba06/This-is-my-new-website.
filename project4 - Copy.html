Petha

Petha is a translucent, soft Indian sweet candy primarily made from ash gourd (also known as winter melon). It's a specialty of Agra, Uttar Pradesh, and is often flavored with rose water and garnished with pistachios. Petha is also known as "Agra ka petha" and has a Geographical Indication (GI) tag.Petha is primarily made from ash gourd (also known as winter melon, white gourd, or raakh laukee), sugar, and sometimes lime water.Petha, a soft, translucent Indian candy, is believed to have originated in the royal kitchens of the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, around 400 years ago. The story goes that he commissioned a sweet as pure and white as the marble Taj Mahal, leading to the creation of petha from ash gourd.
